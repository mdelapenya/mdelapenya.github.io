## Why subscribe?

I'd be lying if I said the subscriber number doesn't matter to me. It does. But not because I'm selling anything.

I write because I enjoy it. I publish because I want to share what I learn. And I want to know who's reading. Not to target you with ads or upsell a course, but to understand what resonates, what topics people care about, and honestly, to get a small ego boost when the number ticks up.

That's it. That's the pitch.

## What you get

When I publish a new post, you get an email with a link to it. One email per post, no batching, no "weekly digest," no "exclusive subscriber content." Just a heads-up that there's something new to read.

## What I do with your email

Nothing else. Specifically:

- I don't sell the list to anyone. Ever.
- I don't share it with sponsors, because there are no sponsors.
- I don't use it for marketing, because there's nothing to market.
- I don't track opens or clicks (Resend offers this, I don't use it).
- There are no ads on this blog and no plans to add them.

The subscriber count is public. The subscriber list is not.

## Where your data lives

Your email is stored by [Resend](https://resend.com), the service that handles the email delivery. You can read their [privacy policy](https://resend.com/legal/privacy-policy). I chose Resend because it's simple, developer-friendly, and handles unsubscribe flows automatically. It also helps that its founder, [Zeno Rocha](https://zenorocha.com/), is a good friend. I even [translated his book to Spanish](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/57215070-14-h-bitos-de-desarrolladores-altamente-productivos).

## How to unsubscribe

Every email I send has an unsubscribe link at the bottom. One click, you're out. No "are you sure?" screens, no guilt trips, no "we'll miss you" emails.
